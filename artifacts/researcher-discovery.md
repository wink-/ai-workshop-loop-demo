## Researcher profile: discovery and framing

Loop engineering is the practice of designing AI workflows as repeatable, inspectable loops instead of one-off prompts. The goal is to turn vague intent into a reliable system that can discover, decide, act, verify, and improve.

This demo uses five building blocks:
1. Discovery — gather the right context and constraints
2. Framing — turn the research into a clear problem statement
3. Planning — break the work into small, testable steps
4. Execution — run the chosen actions or delegated tasks
5. Verification — check results, capture evidence, and refine the loop

For a public GitHub Pages demo, this section will show that the workflow is not just a chat transcript. It is a structured loop that produces traceable outputs, visible decision points, and a clear path from research to outcome. The demo will prove the pattern by walking visitors through the loop from initial question to validated result, making the process understandable, reusable, and easy to adapt.
